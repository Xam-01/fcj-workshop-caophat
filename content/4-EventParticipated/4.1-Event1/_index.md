# FCAJ Community Day 23/5/2026

### Event Objectives

- Update knowledge and new trends about AI and AWS.
- Introduce AWS technologies and services such as Amazon Q and Amazon CloudFront along with practical applications.
- Share experience in product development, participating in hackathons, and deploying real projects.
- Improve understanding of how LLM models and Multi-Agent AI systems operate in enterprise environments.

### Key Highlights

- **In-depth AI & AWS Knowledge Sharing**: The event focused on the latest trends in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and AWS cloud computing, providing attendees with a comprehensive view of how businesses are applying these technologies in practice. Speakers shared their experiences in research, system architecture design, and deploying AI solutions on AWS to solve problems across various industries.
Beyond introducing technologies like Amazon Q, Amazon Bedrock, Amazon CloudFront, and other AWS AI services, the program analyzed the entire pipeline of building a complete AI system. This covered everything from data collection, model training/integration, and cloud deployment to operations, monitoring, and performance optimization. These insights helped attendees better understand how to take an AI idea from the research phase to a production-ready enterprise application.
- **Product Mindset**: A major highlight of the event was a product development mindset oriented toward solving real-world user needs. The speakers emphasized that a product's success relies not just on using cutting-edge technology, but on the ability to correctly identify the problem, build a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) to gather feedback, and continuously iterate.Through real-world examples and successfully deployed projects, attendees learned about the product development lifecycle: from ideation, requirements analysis, and system architecture selection, to feature implementation, testing, and live environment operations.
- **Career Orientation**: The event also offered valuable, practical perspectives on the labor market within Cloud Computing and AI. Experts from GoTymeX, VIB, Cloud Kinetics, VPBank, and FCAJ shared their first-hand experiences.In addition to technical expertise, the speakers heavily emphasized the importance of problem-solving mindsets, self-learning abilities, communication skills, and teamwork. These are considered crucial factors for enhancing competitiveness and achieving sustainable growth in the IT industry.

### Key Takeaways

- **Applying AI Models**: Through the presentations, I gained a clearer understanding of how Large Language Models (LLMs) are applied to real-world tasks such as coding assistance, data analysis, chatbot development, information retrieval, and workflow automation. Instead of just using AI as a Q&A tool, businesses are combining LLMs with databases, APIs, and AWS services to build scalable, intelligent systems that meet diverse needs.
- **Key Concept Clarification**: One of the most valuable lessons was understanding the roles of Context, AI Memory, and the Model Context Protocol (MCP) in improving the quality of AI systems. Context enables AI models to understand user requests more accurately and generate more relevant responses. AI Memory allows systems to retain and reuse information throughout user interactions, creating a more consistent and personalized experience. MCP serves as a bridge between AI models and external tools, APIs, and data sources, significantly expanding the capabilities of enterprise AI systems. These concepts demonstrated that the effectiveness of an AI application depends not only on the language model itself but also on the surrounding architecture and data management strategy.
- **LLM Reasoning Mechanism**: I gained a deeper understanding of how LLMs process information, the non-deterministic nature of their outputs, and how to build Multi-Agent systems to handle complex problems.
- **Hackathon Experience**: I learned how to build a complete product in just 18 hours by optimizing product thinking, ensuring smooth team coordination, managing time effectively, and fully leveraging available tech tools.
  
### Event Experience

What impressed me the most was that every presentation was based on real-world projects rather than theoretical concepts. The speakers demonstrated how companies research, design, deploy, and operate AI systems on cloud platforms. Their experiences in overcoming technical challenges and delivering enterprise solutions provided practical insights that are difficult to gain through classroom learning alone.

Besides acquiring technical knowledge, I also had the opportunity to interact with industry professionals and connect with participants who share similar interests in Cloud Computing and Artificial Intelligence. The Hackathon presentations were especially inspiring, showing how teams successfully built complete products within a limited timeframe. These experiences reinforced the importance of product thinking, teamwork, continuous learning, and proactively keeping up with emerging technologies, motivating me to further develop my knowledge and skills in AWS, Generative AI, and modern software engineering.
